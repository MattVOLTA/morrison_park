## Deal Hypotheses

### 1. Post-Fire Strategic Sale / Recapitalization (Medium Probability)
The May 2024 fire destroyed the Bathurst HQ and distribution center -- the hub for all 11 locations. Rebuilding costs are in the millions. This creates a natural inflection point where the family may:
- Seek a strategic partner to fund the rebuild
- Consider whether rebuilding justifies the capital vs. selling to a larger distributor
- Use insurance proceeds as a catalyst for a recapitalization event

### 2. Roll-Up by Major Distributor (Medium-High Probability)
Ferguson/Wolseley Canada is aggressively acquiring regional HVAC/plumbing/fire distributors in Canada:
- National Fire Equipment (7 locations, Apr 2025)
- Yorkwest Plumbing Supply (4 locations, 2024)
Eddy Group's 11 Maritime locations would give Ferguson/Wolseley instant Atlantic Canada coverage.

### 3. Multi-Branch Family Divergence (Low-Medium Probability)
With ownership spread across descendants of Chesley Eddy's six sons, there may be:
- Differing views on reinvestment vs. liquidity among family branches
- Some family members wanting to cash out after 4-5 generations
- The co-president model (2 non-family, 1 family) may signal professionalization ahead of a sale

### 4. Growth Capital / PE Platform (Low Probability)
PE firms building construction distribution platforms in Atlantic Canada could use Eddy Group as a platform. However, the multi-generational family structure makes PE less likely than a strategic sale.
