## Deal Hypotheses

1. **Growth Capital / Strategic Partnership:** Rodd is actively expanding (MDC Indigenous tourism project, ongoing renovations). A growth capital injection could accelerate property acquisitions or new builds across Atlantic Canada. Mark Rodd appears open to partnerships (MDC advisory board role).

2. **Brand Franchise Conversion:** Major hotel brands (Marriott, Hilton, Choice Hotels) are expanding aggressively in Canada. Rodd's 7 independently branded properties could be attractive for conversion to a franchise model while maintaining family management. This would provide brand recognition, booking systems, and loyalty programs.

3. **Long-Term Succession / Partial Sale (5-10 year horizon):** With no 4th generation visible and Mark in his late 50s/early 60s, a partial or full sale could emerge within the next decade. The 90th anniversary celebrations and legacy focus may signal the beginning of a "legacy preservation" mindset.

4. **Real Estate Value Play:** Rodd owns (likely) the real estate under all 7 properties, including prime locations in Charlottetown, resort land in PEI provincial parks, and waterfront properties. A sale-leaseback or real estate separation could unlock significant value.

5. **Tourism Sector Consolidation:** Atlantic Canada tourism is booming (ACAT investing $30M, record population growth driving investment). A larger hospitality operator (InnVest, Silver Hotel Group) could acquire Rodd as a platform for Maritime expansion.

## Red Flags / Disqualifiers

1. **Not a near-term succession play:** Mark Rodd is relatively young (~58-62) and actively investing and taking on new advisory roles. He shows no signs of stepping back.
2. **Strong emotional attachment:** 91-year family legacy, 90th anniversary celebrations, and David Rodd's emotional pride in Mark's leadership suggest the family may not be willing sellers.
3. **Seasonal business:** Maritime tourism is heavily seasonal. Peak season employees (750+) vs. year-round (139) shows significant seasonality risk.
4. **Government-dependent resort properties:** Brudenell River Resort and Crowbush are within/adjacent to provincial parks, which may involve government leases or concessions that complicate a sale.
5. **Small market size:** Atlantic Canada hospitality is a niche market. Revenue (~$35M) may be below the threshold for institutional PE interest.
