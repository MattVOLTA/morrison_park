## Deal Hypotheses

1. **Founder Retirement / Ownership Transition:** If George Peppin (Chairman) is a founder now in his 70s+, and Andrew Staples is the current owner-operator, the company may be at or past a generational transition. The next transition (from Andrew) could trigger a sale depending on his age and family succession plans.

2. **Strategic Acquisition by Larger Distributor:** National/continental construction product distributors (Beacon Roofing Supply, SRS Distribution) are consolidating the fragmented building products distribution market. Arrow's exclusive supplier relationships and Atlantic Canada coverage make it an attractive bolt-on.

3. **PE Platform or Add-On:** Construction distribution PE firms like CAI Capital Partners, Blue Ridge Construction Capital, or Yellow Point Equity Partners are actively acquiring Canadian construction-related businesses. Arrow's size ($15-25M revenue) fits the lower middle-market sweet spot for PE add-on acquisitions.

4. **Manufacturer Vertical Integration:** Key manufacturers/suppliers with whom Arrow has 25-35 year relationships could acquire their distributor to control the Atlantic Canada channel directly.

5. **Management Buyout:** With a professional management team already in place (CFO Neil Rodgers, VP Sales Matthew McGee), an MBO is plausible if the current owner seeks to exit.

## Red Flags / Disqualifiers

1. **Very small size:** At ~$15-25M revenue and ~30 employees, Arrow may be below the deal size threshold for most institutional investors and strategic acquirers.
2. **Low public profile:** Virtually no media coverage, press releases, or public information. Makes diligence harder and may indicate the owners have no interest in publicity or exit.
3. **Distributor risk:** As a distributor/agent (not manufacturer), value is heavily tied to exclusive supplier relationships. If key vendors shift to other distributors, value erodes.
4. **Seasonal/cyclical:** Atlantic Canada construction is seasonal and tied to government infrastructure spending, which fluctuates.
5. **Aging workforce:** "450+ years combined sales experience" and "300+ years with Arrow" across the sales team implies a deeply tenured but potentially aging workforce. Knowledge transfer risk.
6. **No growth since 2007:** Last branch opening was Saint John in 2007 (19 years ago). This could indicate stable maturity or could signal limited growth appetite.
