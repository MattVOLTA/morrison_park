## 1. Executive Summary

Crosbie Group Limited is one of Newfoundland and Labrador's most storied family enterprises — a 4th-generation business rooted in 1858 that today operates across offshore oil & gas industrial services, marine construction, and commercial/residential real estate. The company is at a pivotal inflection point: founder-family Chairman Rob Crosbie (b. 1957) passed away in April 2025 at age 67, leaving a non-family CEO (Mark Collett) at the helm and an ownership estate in transition. Crosbie has been actively diversifying — acquiring 50% of Nova Scotia's MacKinnon & Olding, launching Panthera Solutions in Guyana, and divesting non-core assets (Crosbie Job Insurance sold to BrokerLink/Intact in June 2025). With revenue estimated at $75M–$115M, 300–500+ employees, and Platinum Best Managed Companies status, this is a sophisticated mid-market platform that could benefit from growth capital advisory, strategic portfolio review, or — depending on family intentions post-Rob's death — a longer-term succession/liquidity conversation.

### 2.2 Company Description

Crosbie Group is a family-owned Newfoundland and Labrador-based conglomerate with an international footprint spanning NL, the Maritimes, the United States, and Guyana. The company operates through two primary divisions: **Industrial Services** (offshore maintenance, scaffolding, coatings, insulation, crane operations, rope access) and **Real Estate** (commercial construction via Chimo Construction, property management, residential development). Through member companies MacKinnon & Olding (Nova Scotia-based marine construction services) and Panthera Solutions (Guyana offshore services JV), Crosbie has diversified beyond its traditional NL offshore base. The company is a Platinum Member of Canada's Best Managed Companies, a recognition it has held consecutively since 2015 and achieved Platinum status in 2022 — [crosbiegroup.com](https://crosbiegroup.com/about-us/).

### 2.3 Products & Services

The death of patriarch Rob Crosbie in April 2025, combined with active divestiture of non-core assets, a non-family CEO already running operations, and unclear 5th-generation succession, creates a natural advisory window. The company is sophisticated enough to appreciate high-quality M&A advice — this is not a naive seller — but the estate transition will inevitably force strategic decisions about the portfolio's future. MPA's value proposition is strongest as a strategic advisor helping the family evaluate options: maintain, grow (Guyana expansion), divest specific divisions, or pursue a full/partial liquidity event.

### 7.3 Deal Hypotheses
Based on this research, potential advisory opportunities:
1. **Estate/Succession Advisory**: With Rob Crosbie's death (Apr 2025), the family needs to make fundamental decisions about the company's future. Multiple siblings/heirs may have different objectives. MPA could advise on options ranging from maintaining the status quo to partial/full liquidity events.
2. **Strategic Portfolio Review**: Crosbie has already started divesting (insurance arm). The remaining portfolio spans industrial services, marine construction (M&O), Guyana JV, and real estate. MPA could advise on optimal portfolio configuration — which pieces to retain, grow, or monetize.
3. **Growth Capital / Acquisition Financing**: If the family decides to double down on growth (Guyana expansion, M&O full acquisition, offshore wind entry), MPA could advise on raising capital or structuring partnerships without diluting family control.
4. **Partial PE Investment**: A private equity minority stake could provide family liquidity while maintaining operational continuity under the strong management team. Attractive to PE given Platinum Best Managed status and diversified revenue base.

### 7.8 Red Flags / Disqualifiers

- **Not a distressed situation**: This is a sophisticated, well-managed company. The family is under no pressure to sell and may prefer to maintain the status quo under professional management.
- **Deep NL institutional identity**: The Crosbie name is synonymous with Newfoundland. Selling "the Crosbie family company" would be culturally significant — the family may resist outright sale for reputational reasons.
- **Active growth mode**: Guyana expansion, M&O growth, offshore wind positioning — Collett has a growth mandate that may not align with a liquidity conversation.
- **Multiple family stakeholders**: Rob had three siblings (Alex, Tim, Cynthia) and a son (Andrew) — estate/ownership may be complex with varying objectives.
- **Potential political sensitivities**: The Crosbie political dynasty (John Crosbie was federal cabinet minister and NL Lieutenant Governor) means any transaction would attract public attention.
