**Ownership Type:** Family-held (founder-owned, 2nd generation entering leadership)

**Founding Story:**
- 1978: Hector Savoie (father) and Jean-Claude Savoie (son) purchased Restigouche Hardwood Ltd. and Cedre Restigouche Ltd. in Saint-Quentin. Hector had been general manager of Southampton Industries' hardwood sawmill in Saint-Quentin. They mortgaged their homes to buy a small sawmill.
- 1980: Major fire destroyed the Restigouche Hardwood sawmill (total loss). Converted the remaining cedar mill to hardwood.
- From near-disaster, Jean-Claude built a vertically integrated hardwood empire over 45+ years.

## Resource Utilization ("125% Efficiency")

The company claims to use "125% of the tree" -- 100% of the sawn material plus 25% from branches, tops, bark, and sawdust converted to biomass fuels. This was articulated by COO Alain Bosse: "When we cut a tree, we use 125% of it. We use 100% of the sawn material, plus 25% as biomass from branches, tops, bark, and sawdust."

This is a marketing/conceptual figure, not a thermodynamic claim. It illustrates their zero-waste approach where every part of harvested trees is converted to a marketable product.

## Deal Hypotheses

### 1. Buy-Side Advisory: Growth Through Acquisition (HIGH Probability)
Groupe Savoie is ACTIVELY acquiring. The Durham Pallet Services deal (Aug 2024) demonstrates appetite and capability. Jason Somers explicitly stated the acquisition strategy: growth without dependence on additional timber supply. They operate 24 pallet repair centers across Canada through their CHEP partnership. MPA could advise on further bolt-on acquisitions in pallet services, hardwood processing, or biomass across Eastern Canada.

### 2. Growth Capital: Capacity Expansion (Medium Probability)
The Westville mill runs at 50% capacity due to timber supply constraints. The company is investing in new sorting technology at Saint-Quentin. Additional capital could fund: (a) expanded procurement programs for private landowner timber, (b) additional processing equipment, (c) Ontario presence buildout post-Durham acquisition. This is a company that reinvests constantly.

### 3. Sell-Side: Eventual Family Exit (Medium-Long Term)
Jean-Claude Savoie is ~75 and the succession transition appears underway. While the company is not currently for sale (it's still acquiring), in 3-5 years the family may consider: (a) sale to a strategic like Interfor or a PE platform, (b) management buyout led by Bosse/Somers, or (c) partial sale retaining family minority. MPA should build the relationship now for future mandates.

### 4. Strategic Advisory: Sustainability/ESG Monetization (Low-Medium Probability)
Groupe Savoie's zero-waste model, PEFC/SFI certifications, and biomass operations position it well for carbon credit markets and ESG-linked financing. Advisory on monetizing sustainability credentials could be valuable.
